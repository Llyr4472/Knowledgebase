Chinese Remainder Theorem

Chinese Remainder Theorem

Oct 16, 20252 min read

Definition

The Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT) solves systems of congruences of the form:

x≡a1​(modn1​)x≡a2​(modn2​)⋯x≡ak​(modnk​)

where all moduli ( n1​, n2​, ..., nk​) are pairwise coprime.

CRT guarantees there is a unique solution modulo:

N=n1​n2​⋯nk​

Formula

  1. Compute the product of moduli:

    N=i=1∏k​ni​

  2. For each congruence:

    • Compute (Mi​=ni​N​)
    • Find the modular inverse:
    yi​≡Mi−1​(modni​)

  3. Construct the solution:

    x≡i=1∑k​ai​⋅Mi​⋅yi​(modN)

Example

Solve:

x≡2(mod3),x≡3(mod5),x≡2(mod7)
  • N=3⋅5⋅7=105
  • For n1​=3 : M1​=35,y1​=35−1(mod3)=2
  • For n2​=5:
    M2​=21,;y2​=21−1(mod5)=1
  • For n3​=7:
    M3​=15,y3​=15−1(mod7)=1

Final solution:

x≡2⋅35⋅2+3⋅21⋅1+2⋅15⋅1≡233≡23(mod105)

Solution: x=23(mod105)
